Projects

Over the past four years I have been dabbling in the world of game development Initially I started by attempting to make a 2D game in Unity that would aim to be a "rogue-like" with inspirations drawn from "The Legend of Zelda, a Link to the Past." However, after some time working on this project I decided to that even though I had become quite good at creating pixel art, that it was not something that I wanted to focus my time on. So, I decided to move into 3D games.

Into Three Dimentions

Creating games in 3D was initially an extremely daunting process, and learning the Unity itself was difficult. However, since I had some experience from creating my first 2D game, this was a bit easier. The idea behind the project was large in scope, I wanted to create an open world game that would allow the player to take full control of other inhabitants of their world. The player would then be able to esentially record paths and jobs of these inhabitants and optimize the efficiency of their town or campsites. The world would contain different types of enemy outposts, bases, and strongholds. These enemies would also mount attacks to raid the players town. This idea while cool, was way outside the scope of what I could create on my own. After several months of development the project had become extremely difficult to work on because there were so many unfinished pieces. Unfortunately, out of frustration I decided to delete the scene and start over. I did this thinking that starting over would allow me to use the parts that I created previously to quickly put things back together in a more orginized way. However, during this reconstruction time I began to get overwhelmed with my University coursework. This led me to forget how exactly everything was put together. In the future, I hope to find time to tackle the project again.

Keep it Simple

My latest attempt in creating a game was much more inside of the scope of my abilities. The idea behind this game is that it would follow the tried and true template of a Top Down RPG, and it would strongly take inspiration from games like "Champions of Norath" and "Gauntlet Legends". The game was coming along nicely, and character and enemy movement were nearly finished in only a few weeks. I was also able to get attacking animations working for the player. The game is still being developed, but I am currently learning the Unreal Engine, and I am planning on rebuilding the project using Unreal.
